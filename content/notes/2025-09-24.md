I was reading a [write-up](https://cacm.acm.org/opinion/ai-and-trust/) of a talk given by
[Bruce Schneier](https://www.schneier.com/) that turned up in the CACM magazine.

He talks about how, because we still use the word "trust" (and adjacent legal concepts) when talking
about the mechanisms that have come to replace interpersonal trust in a larger society, we end up
making a category error. That category error currently manifests in people thinking of corporations
and other organisations as friends, when actually they are services.

Schneier goes on to say:

> Corporations are not moral; they are precisely as immoral as they can get away with.

He then goes on to caution that we are heading towards making the same category error with AI:

> We're going to think of AI as our friend when its not. [..] Did your chatbot recommend a
> particular airline or hotel because it's the best deal for you? Or because the AI company got a
> kickback[..]?
