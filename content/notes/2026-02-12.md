Discord have decided that they're going to require age verification for all users, even though it
has already [leaked a lot of them]. They now claim that they're not going to be collecting any
sensitive information from users.

> For the majority of adult users, we will be able to confirm your age group using information we
> already have.

Given that they don't say _what_ information they've already got, I'm going to assume that it's more
than most people would be comfortable with them having access to.

> When additional confirmation is required, we offer multiple privacy-forward options through
> trusted partners.

The fact that Discord trusts these unnamed partners doesn't mean that I'm going to. I think we all
have a fairly good idea of what corporate trust actually means. The go on to assert that they're not
going to actually receive any of the information used to verify our ages:

> Facial scans never leave your device. Discord and our vendor partners never receive it.
>
> IDs are used to get your age only and then deleted.
>
> Discord only receives your age — that’s it. Your identity is never associated with your account.

Discord are asserting that their "trusted" partners are not going to be receiving any information.
Surely these parters would never adjust their own policies to include collection of this
information.

I'm not at all convinced that this is going to be a good idea. I think that there are much easier
ways of assuring users identity (or age, in this case). I have an Apple iPhone, and I'm quite
certain that Apple know how old I am. Moreover, they could provide an API that allows identity
providers to provide proof of my identity, perhaps borrowing from Zero-Knowledge Proofs.
