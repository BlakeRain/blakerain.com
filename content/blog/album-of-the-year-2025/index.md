# Dedication

At the end of September I learned about the passing of an old friend of mine [Ian Backhouse], known
online for as long as I can remember as _Runewolf_. We'd fallen out of touch for the past couple of
years, only chatting on Discord once every now and then. In 2025 he stopped responding. Whilst it
was not entirely unusual for him to sometimes take a while to reply to me, this time it turned out
he had passed away in June.

It was Ian who introduced me to metal in my teens. I still remember the first mix tape he made me
(_tape!_). The first track was something like Rhapsody's [Rain of a Thousand Flames], and I was
instantly hooked. I spent a lot of weekends over at his house, or meeting in Borders bookshop on
Briggate in Leeds. Online I knew Ian as Runewolf on IRC: the `#truemetalmp3` channel on Undernet. It
was with Ian and friends that I first travelled to [Wacken] for the awesome metal festival there.

As is often the way, Ian and I drifted apart over the last few years, especially with my move down
to the south of England. Even so, it was still quite a shock to find out that he had passed away in
June. Finding his obituary site, and seeing my 19-20 year old self in some of the photos there, was
a real blast of nostalgia, now twinged with sadness.

Ian was a wonderfully sarcastic and often exquisitely disdainful bastard, who's whit and
intelligence were a significant part of my late teens and early adult years. He was supposed to
outlive us all.
